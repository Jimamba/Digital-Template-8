Race to 21

  • High Concept: A luck-based indirect-control based game, where two players try to get to 21 before the other player.
  • Player Experience Goals: The experience goal I was aiming for is to give players a sense of importance when it comes to timing.
  • Genre: Indirect control, 1v1 competition.
  • Game play: The controls are simple, each player has a turn. They must time when to stop the cycle of numbers, in order to get a certain number of points. To pause the timer they must press S. Unfortunately, I don't think I got this to work right. The reason the game is so simplistic in it's control and design, is because we were supposed to make a game that's different from what we usually make. Since most of my games involve total control over the character/main gameplay points, I had to make a game with the least amount of player control as possible.
  • Prototype goal: Indirect-Control, Luck-Based/Reaction-time based gameplay.
  • Features: It's so far my only indirect control styled game, and it requires a good reaction time for the player to win.
  • Setting/Premise: There is not setting or premise.
  • Story: There is no story either.
  • Target Audience: People that like to compete against each other without complex rules.
  • Play time: Approximately 15 seconds.
  • Strategies: Timing and carefully watching the cycle of numbers.
